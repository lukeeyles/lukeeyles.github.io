Research

As part of my degree I am undertaking a capstone project, which is a major engineering project that I am planning and executing independently, under the guidance of an academic supervisor. The project I am working on is to research and develop an improved control system for an exoskeleton arm, which consists of two parts: the inverse kinematics and the admittance controller.

Planning

In the planning stage, I first performed background research to analyse the problem’s context and review the literature on the exoskeleton arm control systems. I then developed a detailed project management plan, including a Gantt chart to plan out the time allocated to each stage of the project, and a risk analysis to prepare for uncertainties. The final part of the planning stage was a research proposal, in which I expanded on my literature review, proposed methods of evaluating the project, finalised the proposed timeline, and reflected on the progress of the project up until this point.

Implementation

I am currently early in the implementation stage of this project. So far, I have set up my ROS development environment, have built a simulated model of the robot using Xacro to generate the robot description file, and have used KDL to model the robot’s kinematics and calculate the Jacobian for the inverse kinematics.

I have also set up a kinematic model of the robot in MATLAB, to enable fast testing of potential solutions in simulation before I implement then in C++ with ROS.

Teamwork

For the major project in Mechanical and Mechatronic Design, my team had the opportunity to work with Thales researching underwater robot communication and localisation. Delivery of this project was a great success, and proved to be a significant learning experience for what makes an effective team.

The key factors I learned that contributed to the success of the team were:

  • Good project planning, with a clear specification, timeline, and set goals for each sprint. This planning was key to the success of the project, as we planned a scoping stage, researching several potential solutions, which gave us the flexibility we needed to pivot to another option when the requirements of the project changed.
  • Frequent meetings to review work done, ask any questions, and work on areas of the project together. Meetings created an open environment where everyone could share ideas and collaboratively problem solve, achieving a better outcome than working alone.
  • Committed team members, with agreed upon responsibilities for each member. Defined responsibilities help give each person a sense of ownership over the project, so that all team members were invested in the success of the project.
