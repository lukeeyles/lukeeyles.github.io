My Academic Projects

These are some of the projects I've worked on in my engineering degree.

Tic-Tac-Toe Bot

For my major project in the university subject Robotics, I led a team with two others to successfully program a DOBOT Magician to play Tic-Tac-Toe against a person.

Achievements:

  • Achieved the goal of the project by successfully programming a robot to play tic-tac-toe with a person.
  • Utilised my problem solving skills to manage a complex project and integrate many different components, including the inverse kinematics of the robots, program logic, and image processing to detect how the player had moved.
  • Demonstrated initiative by going outside of the scope of the subject to include image processing in the project, so that the robot would recognise how the player had moved. This required investigating MATLAB’s image processing capabilities and learning how to implement the best option.

Learnings:

  • Coordinating the team and making sure everyone had a part to work on was a challenge, and as a result I learned that it is best to assign team members clearly defined roles and hold regular team meetings, to ensure that everyone feels a sense of accountability for their part of the project.
  • Learnt that when working with a physical robot, extra time must be given when planning to account for difficulties caused by physical limitations, such as joint limits and joint positioning error.

TurtleBot Finding Cereal

For the major project in the subject Sensors and Control, I successfully led a team with two others to program a TurtleBot to look for cereal boxes, recognise the chosen box, and drive along the normal vector of the box.

Achievements:

  • Demonstrated leadership skills by working early in the project to plan the code structure and divide the work among the team. This ensured the success of the project as everyone was on the same page from the beginning, and everyone had a specific part of the project that they were responsible for.
  • Applied learnings from the subject to successfully recognise the boxes, used the pinhole camera model together with depth information from the 3D camera to locate their position, and control the robot to drive to the boxes.

Simulated Aircraft Interceptor

In Programming for Mechatronic Systems at university, a project I undertook was to use C++ with ROS to predict the trajectory of enemy aircraft and intercept them in a simulated 2D airspace.

Achievements:

  • Exceeded the threshold for bonus marks by 50%, averaging over 60 intercepted within 5 minutes.
  • Demonstrated creative thinking by developing an iterative algorithm which predicted the point to intercept.

Warman Competition 2019

As part of the Mechanical Design 1 subject, I worked in a team of 5 to create a robot to participate in the 2019 Warman Design and Build Competition. The goal of the challenge was to design a robot to lift plastic balls from one container and transport them to another container 2 metres away.

Achievements:

  • Demonstrated innovative thinking and teamwork by brainstorming with the team to develop a unique method to lift the balls, by rolling a large adhesive wheel across them to pick them up.
  • Planned around the constraints of using an open loop control system by designing the robot with a differential drive system, so that the robot’s movements could be calculated precisely and it would drive reliably.

Learnings:

  • Learnt the importance of including time for testing and troubleshooting in project planning, as all components of the robot worked individually, but we had difficulty integrating them to work together before the deadline.

Wall Building Robots

As part of the university subject Robotics, I programmed simulated UR3 and UR5 robot arms to work together to build a wall, using MATLAB with Peter Corke’s Robotics Toolbox.

Achievements:

  • Applied my learnings from the subject by using inverse kinematics to find waypoints for the robots to pick up and place the bricks, and quintic polynomial trajectory planning to smoothly interpolate between these waypoints.
  • Increased efficiency by designing the system so that the robots picked up the bricks at the same time, while avoiding collisions by taking turns to place the bricks.

Arduino Security System

I programmed an Arduino security system that used a distance sensor mounted to a stepper motor to scan the room and detect any changes, as part of the university subject Mechatronics 2. The system had many features, one key feature being able to print a map of the room.

Achievements:

  • Utilised careful planning to design the program in a modular way, so it could be easily modified to add any features required. This expedited the development process, and ensured I was able to implement all features by the deadline.
  • Applied my problem solving skills to work around the memory limitations of the Arduino, by creating a method to print the map of the room which printed each cell as it was calculated, saving memory.
