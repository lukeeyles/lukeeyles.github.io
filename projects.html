My Projects

These are some of the projects I've worked on in my engineering degree.

Tic-Tac-Toe Bot

For my major project in the university subject Robotics, I led a team with two others to successfully program a DOBOT Magician to play Tic-Tac-Toe. This project was complex, and utilised my problem solving skills to integrate many different components, including the inverse kinematics of the robots, program logic, and image processing to detect how the player had moved.

This project was a huge learning experience, as I had never worked with a physical robot before, and had to deal with physical limitations such as joint limits and positioning error. My team also pushed outside of the scope of the subject by including image processing, which required investigating MATLAB’s image processing capabilities and learning how to implement the best option.

TurtleBot Finding Cereal

For the major project in the subject Sensors and Control, I successfully led a team with two others to program a TurtleBot to look for cereal boxes, recognise the chosen box, and drive along the normal vector of the box. My leadership skills were demonstrated in this project, as I worked early on to plan the code structure and divide the work among the team. This ensured the success of the project as everyone was on the same page from the beginning, and everyone had a specific part of the project that they were responsible for.

Simulated Aircraft Interceptor

In Programming for Mechatronic Systems at university, a project I undertook was to use C++ with ROS to predict the trajectory of enemy aircraft and intercept them in a simulated 2D airspace. Bonus marks were awarded if more than 40 were intercepted within 5 minutes, and my program exceeded this by 50%, averaging over 60 intercepted. I did this by utilising my problem solving skills to develop an iterative prediction algorithm, where the system calculated an estimate of the time to intercept based on how far away the aircraft was, then extrapolated the aircraft’s position by that time and repeated the process until the value converged.

Warman Competition 2019

As part of the Mechanical Design 1 subject, I worked in a team of 5 to create a robot to participate in the 2019 Warman Design and Build Competition. The goal of the challenge was to design a robot to lift plastic balls from one container and transport them to another container 2 metres away. To lift the balls, the method my team developed was to roll a large adhesive wheel across them, so that the balls would stick to the wheel. The shaft of the wheel was mounted to a pin joint so that it could be lifted in and out of the container, and a differential drive system was used to drive the robot so that its motions could be controlled predictably.
